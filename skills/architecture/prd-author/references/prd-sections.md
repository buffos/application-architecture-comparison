# PRD Sections

Use this structure unless the user clearly needs a variant.

## 1. Title

State the product name or working title.

## 2. Purpose

Explain:

- why this product exists
- why this document exists
- what planning or implementation work it enables

## 3. Product Summary

Give a compact definition of the product and the core business workflow.

## 4. Product Goals

List the primary outcomes the product must achieve.

## 5. Non-Goals

State what the product intentionally does not solve in the current scope.

## 6. Users or Actors

Define the key roles that interact with the system and what they do.

## 7. Domain Scope

List the major business objects or conceptual areas without overcommitting to implementation shape.

## 8. Core Business Capabilities

Describe the main capabilities the product must support.

## 9. Required Business Rules

Capture policies, thresholds, hard invariants, and exceptions that materially shape the product.

## 10. Required Workflows

Describe end-to-end flows:

- happy path
- approval/exception path
- failure or shortage path
- return/reversal path

## 11. Functional Requirements

Express concrete product requirements grouped by capability.

## 12. Non-Functional Requirements

Capture constraints like:

- repeatability
- simplicity of local delivery
- testability
- determinism
- observability

## 13. Acceptance Scenarios

Write scenario-style requirements that later artifacts and tests can reference.

## 14. Optional Comparative or Educational Framing

If the product exists to compare architectures, include a section that explains why the chosen scope is a good comparison surface.
